Born on 15th October,1961 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr.Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and the President of India

Kalam was the youngest of five siblings and was noted for his integrity and his simple lifestyle.A well-known scientist associated with Kalam, Dr Raghunath Mashelkar, told media after his death:"The very first meeting touched me, because I saw a man who was simple, very humane and yet professional at the same time. I saw evidence of his extraordinary humility and willingness to learn from others in another instance."

Kalam was a great motivator too. His words, "Look at the sky. We are not alone. The whole universe is friendly to us and conspires only to give the best to those who dream and work" persuades students to think high and dream big. He is the author of various best sellers such as:

  • Wings of Fire
  • Ignited Minds
  • India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium

Kalam's vision was to transform the nation into a developed country from developing one. His contibutions to the scientific world brought glory to the country. Some of his contributions are:

  • Development of Agni and Prithvi
  • Coordination of Pokhran-II and SLV III
  • Invention of Kalam-Raju - a tablet to monitor health of underprivileged people in rural India are few of his great contributions.

Kalam was awarded India's highest civilian honour, Bharat Ratna,for his contribution to the scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India. The death of this prolific personality was a loss not just to the scientific community, but to the country as a whole.

"Unless India stands up to the world, no one will respect us. In this world, fear has no place. Only strength respects strength."

-- A.P.J Abdul Kalam
